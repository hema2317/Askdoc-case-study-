01 Overview

The numbers behind the need

Health illiteracy costs the US healthcare system $238 billion annually. Most people leave doctor appointments confused — and turn to Google, which gives them anxiety instead of answers.

88%
of US adults have below-proficient health literacy
$238B
annual cost of health illiteracy to the US economy
72%
of patients Google symptoms before or after appointments
4.2×
more likely to be hospitalized when health literacy is low
⚕
The Problem
People receive lab results, diagnoses, and medication instructions they don't understand. They can't afford specialist time for every question. The gap between "what the doctor said" and "what I actually understand" is where health crises begin.
→
The Opportunity
AI can serve as a personal health translator — one that knows your history, speaks plain language, and helps you arrive at your doctor's office with the right questions. AskDoc+ fills the gap between appointment and anxiety.

05 Design System

Tokens, type, and trust

Every visual decision in AskDoc+ was made to communicate trust. Healthcare UI demands a different kind of clarity — no dark patterns, no alarm signals without reason, no ambiguity about what the AI can and cannot do.

06 Final Design

Every screen, every decision

The final designs reflect a single design philosophy: make the complex feel calm. Healthcare doesn't need to feel clinical or scary — it needs to feel like a knowledgeable friend.

01

The Chat Interface — Where Trust Begins

The first thing users see is the medical disclaimer. Rather than hiding it, we made it a moment — a full-screen card that pauses and asks for acknowledgment. This isn't friction; it's a trust signal that sets the right expectations before any interaction occurs.

"I Understand" button — affirms comprehension, not just closure
Persistent footer disclaimer on every chat screen — regulatory compliance without interruption
"AskDoc is thinking…" loading state — humanizes the AI without overpromising
Free-text input, no categories — users describe symptoms in their own words
02

Response & Full Analysis — Two Depths of Information

Users get two layers: a brief, scannable summary in the chat bubble, and a deeper Full Medical Analysis report accessible on demand. This respects both the "just tell me quickly" user and the "I want to understand everything" user.

Chat summary surfaces possible issue + urgency level at a glance
Full Report opens a modal with: Your Input, Summary, Possible Issue, Why This May Be Happening, What You Should Do
IBS cross-reference in the response signals the AI "knows" the user — drives trust and retention
Urgency banner in green keeps the experience calm even when surfacing health concerns
03

Health Memory — The Product's True Differentiator

No other consumer health app builds a persistent, AI-extracted memory from every user interaction. The Health Profile screen was designed to feel like a health journal that builds itself — organized, filterable, and doctor-ready.

4-stat header (Memories, Priority, Conditions, Sources) gives immediate sense of depth
Filter chips (All, High, Lab, Chat, Photo) let users find the right memory in one tap
Source icon (📄 Lab Report, 💬 Chat, 📷 Photo) makes origin immediately scannable
Profile screen captures blood type, smoking, alcohol, exercise — the inputs that make AI personalization possible
04

The Paywall — Earn Upgrade, Don't Demand It

The upgrade screen was designed with a specific philosophy: show value before price. The headline is about the user's experience ("Your Personal AI Health Assistant"), the features are written as scenarios ("Is this rash serious? — ask unlimited times"), and the free tier is shown first — signaling confidence, not desperation.

Benefit-first copywriting — scenarios over feature names
Free plan shown prominently — reduces anxiety, increases conversion trust
Annual plan shows "Only $7.50/month" — reframes anchoring to daily cost
"MOST POPULAR" badge on Pro monthly — social proof without manufactured urgency
Exit-intent discount modal designed (not shown) for users who close without converting

08 Outcomes & Learnings

What shipped. What we learned.

AskDoc+ is live on the iOS App Store, peer-reviewed, and being iterated actively. Here's what the design process delivered — and what it taught.

AMI
Peer-reviewed publication in Applied Medical Informatics, Vol. 48 No. 1, March 2026
3
Core interaction modes — Chat, Lab Upload, Photo Scan — covering the full symptom-to-understanding journey
HIPAA
Fully compliant data architecture with Supabase, zero PHI sent to external AI without consent
⚖️
Compliance is a design constraint, not an afterthought
Apple's health guidelines and HIPAA requirements shaped every screen. The medical disclaimer modal, footer banner, and educational framing weren't legal add-ons — they were UX decisions that built user trust.
🧠
Personalization is the product's moat
The moment users saw their IBS referenced in a response about leg tingling, retention intent spiked. The health memory isn't a feature — it's the reason to stay. Everything else is table stakes.
🎯
Calm wins over clinical
Early testing showed users wanted reassurance, not information density. We stripped urgency language, added the "Low/Medium/High" urgency system, and rewrote AI prompts to lead with context before concern.
